FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best time to visit Shimla for snowfall? +
Shimla receives snowfall primarily from December to February. The heaviest snowfall is usually in January. If you want to experience a snowy Shimla, visit between December 25 and February 15.
How many days are enough to explore Shimla? +
3 to 4 days are ideal to cover all major attractions in Shimla including The Ridge, Mall Road, Jakhu Temple, Christ Church, Viceregal Lodge, and a day trip to Kufri. If you want to explore nearby areas like Chail or Narkanda, extend to 5–6 days.
Are private vehicles allowed on Mall Road? +
No. Private vehicles are strictly prohibited on Mall Road and The Ridge area. You must park at designated parking areas like Khalini Parking or use the local taxi/auto service to reach the main bazaar.
What is the weather like in Shimla during summer? +
Summers (March–June) are the most popular time to visit. Temperatures range from 15°C to 25°C during the day and drop to 8–12°C at night. It's pleasantly cool compared to the plains, making it a perfect summer escape.
Is Shimla safe for solo female travellers? +
Yes, Shimla is generally considered very safe for solo female travellers. The town has a strong police presence and tourist police. The locals are friendly and helpful. Exercise normal precautions as you would in any tourist destination.
What local food should I try in Shimla? +
Must-try dishes include Chha Gosht, Dham (traditional Himachali feast), Madra, Bhey, Babru (stuffed puri), and Siddu (steamed bread). For sweets, try Mittha (sweet rice) and Khatta. Baljees on Mall Road is one of the best local restaurants.
